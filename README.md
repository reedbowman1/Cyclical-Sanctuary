# Cyclical-Sanctuary
Visuals for Term 2 Project for MUSC 419

Our project is based on the life cycle of a flower. In the piece, the dancer resembles the different phases that a flower goes through: seed in a barren landscape, rain, grass blooming, sprouting in the grass, blossoming, flooding, and drought. In this piece, we have different audio and visual elements to accompany each section, and the end of the piece occurs at the beginning, making it cyclical. 
	The first section is a drought, which is embodied by the sparse particles on the screen and reverberated synths with wind sound effects. In this section, the dancer’s velocity controls the number of particles on the screen.
	The second section is bloom, where the sparse particles fill in, and grass takes over the dry background. As this occurs, a flower sprouts from the grass, which is represented by chimes in the audio and a yellow circle on the screen. This yellow circle grows as the dancer’s hand moves upwards, resembling a flower sprouting. The dancer’s hand y-axis also controls the low-pass filter of the bells. Once the flower sprouts, it blooms into a full flower, which is represented by the image of a sunflower, and an abundant backing track. As this track and flowers become more abundant, the visuals show more, and rain creeps in.
	As the rain creeps in, it leads to the third section: storm. As the storm comes in, the music gets more chaotic and the background more turbulent. The wind is controlled by the dancer’s hand and moves as she does. The track becomes more chaotic and discordant. Dampened bass drum sounds will be triggered based on dancer’s velocity to further represent this chaos. As these microtones swell and rain floods the screen, the flowers slowly decay until it is just rain and wind.
The fourth section is the drought again, as a consequence of the rapid storm. After the storm followed by a moment of silent aftermath, the land is back to the deserted stage just like the beginning. The same dancer movement control applies, using velocity to control the density of land-representing particles.
	The final section is a repeat of the bloom section, which solidifies the cyclicality of the piece. As the grass finally grows, the flower begins to bloom, and the piece abruptly ends where it began: completing the cycle, leaving room for the audience to continue imagining their garden journey.
